History

White water rafting began as a practical way for early explorers and traders to travel through fast-moving rivers. In the mid-1800s, wooden rafts were first used on major expeditions across rough waters in North America. As technology improved, rubber rafts were introduced in the 1940s, making the activity safer and more accessible.
